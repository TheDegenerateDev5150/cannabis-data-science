%Testing lab perspective here:   I can attest that very few lab test .csv files are accepted by CCRS and 100\% of the errors are because of "Invalid Inventory IDs" meaning the licensee has not reported that item as part of their Inventory report. ~sometimes~ on the 2nd or 3rd upload try over the course of a couple of weeks, I will get a file that "goes through" and isn't rejected, but more than 50\% of all of our lab data just isn't getting into the system. Ever. I have also confirmed multiple times with all our clients and their software integrators and they all say those inventory numbers are getting uploaded, but the files still keep getting rejected..... We do not have a 3rd party integrator complete our uploads, we drag/drop the .csv files manually into the LCB's interface. The inventory identifiers we use are copied/pasted directly from the manifest from the client, so it's up to them to ensure that's the lot number they upload into their Inventory reports. Ideally BEFORE the testing is done so I don't have to make multiple attempts.   When I ask the LCB to confirm if they're receiving any files from us, or what I might do to fix this, they ask me for which licensees this is for - I have not responded to their requests because I don't want enforcement going after my clients, but as David suggests - what options are left for me to be in compliance? Is it CCRS that's broken? Am I getting incorrect error messages? or are all these clients--who I trust--and their software integrators just not telling the truth? I have not been able to get clarity from the LCB on what my responsibility is as a lab to try and get these files uploaded.....if the client says they've been reported, and it keeps getting rejected, who's responsible for the lab test data!?..... I have spent HUNDREDS of hours of my time in the past year dealing with CCRS and my success rate for lab test uploads has only increased minutely. *I would like to see more people be more vocal about how terrible and dysfunctional this system is......e.g. testing labs: *how many of your test result files are NOT rejected? How often do you re-attempt uploads and for how long? B2B transfer folks? If we all continue to look the other way, things will not just magically fix themselves.
